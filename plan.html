The Full Plan

Everything we'd build,
and why.

The review page covered what's happening now. This is the whole map: every piece worth building, what each one does for you, and the order I'd do it in.

You do not have to do all of this at once, and you shouldn't. This is the full picture so you can see where it's headed. We'd start with Phase 1 and move at whatever pace and budget make sense for you. Skim the headers, dig into whatever you want.

Phase1

Foundation & first fixes

Stop the leaks and get measurement in place, so nothing's slipping and we can prove what's working from here on.

✓

Fix the urgent bugs

The footer phone link, the logo link, and the invisible form text. The things costing you calls right now.

✓

Turn on tracking

Google Analytics, Search Console, and conversion tracking on forms, calls, and purchases. So every decision after this is based on real numbers, not guesses.

✓

Write your Google listings

Real headline and summary text on every page, so Google shows what you want, not scraps it picked itself.

✓

Set a proper share image

So a shared link shows your shop, not a blank map pin.

✓

Optimize your Google Business Profile

The map listing that drives the most calls. Categories, hours, photos, services, and products all filled in.

✓

Fix the reviews

Replace the "Grass Luvers" quotes with real ones, and start asking every happy customer for a review.

✓

Map the redirects

Match every old page address to its new one before we move platforms, so you keep whatever search standing you've built.

Phase2

The new store on Shopify

Rebuild on Shopify with a custom, hand-coded theme, so it's fast, it's yours, and you can run it yourself without a developer.

✓

Custom-coded theme

Built to match your brand and coded properly, not thrown together in a drag-and-drop builder that bogs the site down.

✓

Your full catalog online

The mowers, plus everything you actually move: oils, filters, blades, belts, trimmer line, parts, and every brand you carry.

✓

Real categories, filters, and search

So a customer (and Google) can actually find the specific thing they came for.

✓

Pickup or ship at checkout

Small items get a buy button with both options. Big equipment stays shown-online, sold-in-person, no card fees on a $14,000 machine.

✓

Real photos and specs on every item

Plus product reviews turned on. Real photos sell better and cut returns.

✓

Tax, shipping, and policies set up

Indiana sales tax, shipping rates, and your warranty, returns, shipping, and privacy pages, all handled correctly.

✓

Move your eBay listings over

Bring them onto your own store where the fees are far lower, and point your eBay buyers to your site.

Phase3

Getting found

Make sure the people searching for what you sell and fix land on you, not the big box stores.

✓

A page for every brand

Husqvarna, Scag, Echo, Hustler. People search by brand, and right now you show up for almost none of them.

✓

A page for every service

Sales, repair, blade sharpening, pickup and delivery. Each one can rank on its own for searches the big stores can't touch.

✓

Local pages for the towns you serve

Columbus, Seymour, North Vernon, and the rest, so you turn up in "near me" searches across the area.

✓

Structured data for star ratings

The behind-the-scenes code that lets Google show your 4.3 stars and other rich details next to your name.

✓

Manufacturer dealer listings

Get listed in the "find a dealer" tools on Scag, Husqvarna, and Echo's sites. Free traffic and a link Google trusts.

✓

Directory and map listings

Apple Maps, Bing, Yelp, Facebook, all with matching name, address, and phone so Google trusts you more.

✓

A review engine

A simple routine to ask every customer, reply to all of them, and keep fresh reviews coming. Recent reviews push you up.

✓

Real photography across the site

Swap any stock or AI images for real shots of your shop, team, and equipment. Better trust, better ranking signals.

✓

Seasonal how-to posts

"When to sharpen your blades," "winterizing your mower." The exact things people search that pull them to your site.

Phase4

Repeat business & reach

Turn one-time buyers into regulars, and get in front of new people. This is where a fixed site becomes a growing one.

✓

Email capture and a welcome note Repeat revenue

With a proper opt-in, so you can actually reach these people later, legally.

✓

Service-reminder emails Repeat revenue

Automatic "time to winterize" and "spring tune-up" reminders. Repeat business on autopilot, and one of the biggest wins for an equipment shop.

✓

Seasonal sales and cart reminders

Simple campaigns for your busy seasons, plus a nudge to anyone who left something in their cart.

✓

Financing shown on big equipment

Many buyers say yes to a $10,000 machine when they see a monthly number. Showing it upfront closes more sales.

✓

A "text us" option

Let people text a quick question. Local shops convert really well on text, and most customers prefer it to calling.

✓

Active Facebook and Instagram

New arrivals, before-and-after repairs, seasonal tips. Keeps you top of mind and feeds people back to the site.

✓

YouTube how-to and demo videos

Short videos on starting, maintaining, and choosing equipment. They rank in Google too, so they pull in new people.

✓

Paid ads, when you're ready Optional

Google Local Services and retargeting for high-intent local searches. Only once the foundation is earning, not before.

That's the whole map.

It's a lot on one page, but it's not a lot at once. We'd start with Phase 1, get the first fixes and the store live, and build the rest at the pace that fits your budget. You'd own all of it, and you'd be able to run it yourself.
